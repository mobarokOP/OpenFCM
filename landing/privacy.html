Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 13, 2026

OneFCM is a push notification platform for Android apps. It comes in two flavors: open-source software you run on your own server, and a hosted cloud service we operate at onefcm.com. This policy explains what data is involved in each, how it's used, and what control you have over it. We've tried to keep it short and readable — if anything is unclear, just ask.

Information we collect

When you create an account on the hosted service, we collect your e-mail address and name so we can sign you in, secure your account and contact you about the service.

For each app you connect, the OneFCM SDK registers the devices that install it. Per device we store:

  • the FCM registration token used to deliver notifications;
  • device metadata such as device model, Android version, app version, language and country;
  • any tags or user identifiers the app developer chooses to attach for targeting.

We do not require, and ask that app developers do not send us, sensitive personal data in tags or notification payloads.

How we use it

Device data exists for exactly two purposes: delivering notifications to the right devices, and providing delivery analytics (sent, delivered, opened) to the owner of the app that registered them. We don't build advertising profiles, we don't track end users across apps, and we don't use device data for anything beyond operating the service.

Data ownership

Self-hosted: if you run OneFCM on your own server, all data lives in your own database. It is 100% yours; we have no access to it and this policy's cloud sections simply don't apply.

Cloud: data for apps on the hosted service is stored on OneFCM servers. It remains yours — we act as a processor on your behalf, and we never sell it or share it with third parties for their own purposes.

Firebase / Google

Notification delivery transits Firebase Cloud Messaging, Google's push delivery network, using the Firebase project you connect to each app. That means message payloads and registration tokens pass through Google's infrastructure and are handled under Google's terms and privacy commitments in addition to ours.

Data retention & deletion

Device records and analytics are kept while the associated app exists. Deleting an app removes its devices, delivery logs and analytics; deleting your account removes everything associated with it, including your profile data. Backups age out on a rolling schedule shortly after. You can also request deletion by e-mail at any time.

Security

Firebase service account files you upload are stored encrypted, API keys are stored hashed (we can't read them back, and neither can anyone else), and all traffic to the dashboard and API goes over HTTPS. No system is perfectly secure, but we follow common-sense engineering practice and fix reported issues quickly — the codebase is open source, so you're welcome to look.

Contact

Questions, deletion requests or concerns: hello@onefcm.com. If we update this policy, we'll change the effective date above and note material changes on this page.
